Simulation of an AI-Driven IoT Street Lighting System

Researcher: Agbama Godwin Ebene

Department: Computer Science

Year: 2025

Research Area: Smart Cities / IoT / AI

Background of the Study

Street lighting plays a vital role in urban safety, but traditional systems consume excessive energy. This project proposes a simulation-based design of smart lighting systems that leverage IoT sensors and AI models to adapt lighting conditions based on real-time traffic data.

Problem Statement

Conventional lighting systems lead to power waste. This project addresses the high energy costs and carbon emissions by simulating a smarter, AI-controlled alternative using network and AI tools.

Aim and Objectives

  • Review smart lighting and AI optimization methods.
  • Design and simulate an intelligent lighting system in MATLAB.
  • Evaluate energy efficiency and system responsiveness.
  • Propose a real-world deployment plan based on findings.

Methodology

  • Data from open sources (Kaggle, transport agencies).
  • Simulate IoT lighting network in MATLAB.
  • Apply machine learning for adaptive control.
  • Compare results with traditional lighting systems.

Significance

Provides a low-cost pathway to smart lighting for developing regions. Supports smart city goals by showing feasibility before real deployment.

Scope

  • Focuses on simulation, not physical implementation.
  • Excludes hardware deployment challenges.
